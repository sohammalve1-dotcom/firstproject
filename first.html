Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer, born on December 28, 1969, in Helsinki. He is best known for creating the Linux operating system kernel in 1991 while studying at the University of Helsinki. His work laid the foundation for one of the world's most widely used open-source operating systems. In 2005, he also developed Git, a version control system that has become an essential tool for software development. Today, Linus Torvalds continues to oversee the development of the Linux kernel and is recognized as one of the most influential figures in the history of computing and open-source software.

Achievements

  • 1991: Created the Linux kernel and released its first version.
  • 1994: Released Linux 1.0, the first stable version of the Linux operating system.
  • 2005: Developed Git, the version control system used by millions of developers.
  • 2012: Received the Millennium Technology Prize for creating Linux.
  • 2014: Received the IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award for his contributions to computer science
  • Present: Continues to lead the development of the Linux kernel, which powers billions of devices worldwide.
